# Brief: US Iran War

## Bottom Line
The U.S.–Iran war that began on February 28, 2026 has produced simultaneous ceasefire diplomacy, a U.S. naval blockade of Iranian ports, and a contested Strait of Hormuz. The IEA calls the energy shock the largest in history. India—highly dependent on Gulf energy and with equities in Chabahar—faces sustained supply, sanctions, and maritime‑risk exposure. ([centcom.mil](https://www.centcom.mil/MEDIA/PRESS-RELEASES/Press-Release-View/Article/4457255/us-to-blockade-ships-entering-or-exiting-iranian-ports/))

## Key Takeaways
- The U.S. blockade (from April 13) and Iran’s counter‑measures have created a “dual restriction” at Hormuz, intermittently punctuated by U.S. escort operations (“Project Freedom”). ([centcom.mil](https://www.centcom.mil/MEDIA/PRESS-RELEASES/Press-Release-View/Article/4457255/us-to-blockade-ships-entering-or-exiting-iranian-ports/))
- India’s exposure is acute: ~88–89% crude import dependence, thin SPR (~5.33 MMT, ~64% filled), and dozens of India‑bound ships affected; the Navy is escorting under Op Sankalp. ([indianexpress.com](https://indianexpress.com/article/explained/explained-economics/indias-reliance-on-imported-oil-may-hit-fresh-high-in-fy26-10548802/?utm_source=openai))
- Chabahar remains strategic but sanctions risk rose after the U.S. revoked the 2018 waiver; a temporary Treasury letter covered activity only until April 26, 2026. ([sansad.in](https://sansad.in/getFile/loksabhaquestions/annex/186/AU1103_r6YhID.pdf?source=pqals))

## Strategic Context
- Epic Fury’s initial strikes killed Khamenei and hit Iranian military infrastructure; Iran retaliated region‑wide; UNSC 2817 condemned Iranian regional attacks; a Hormuz security resolution later failed due to vetoes. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/8de8054f3abd4688f894c657467ee3dd?utm_source=openai))
- IAEA reports no initial nuclear‑site damage but insists any end‑of‑war deal must embed robust verification. ([investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/world-news/no-sign-irans-nuclear-sites-were-hit-iaea-says-but-iran-alleges-one-was-4533937?utm_source=openai))

## Implications
- Energy: Expect prolonged premium on oil/LNG delivered to India until Hormuz fully normalizes; OMC under‑recoveries and fiscal measures likely persist. ([business-standard.com](https://www.business-standard.com/economy/news/government-cuts-fuel-excise-duty-imposes-export-levy-on-diesel-atf-126032700327_1.html?utm_source=openai))
- Maritime security: Indian Navy convoying and granular DGS routing/advisories will be essential for risk management during partial openings. ([dgshipping.gov.in](https://www.dgshipping.gov.in/WriteReadData/News/202602280342249592926MARSECDGSCircular08of2026dated28Feb20262.pdf?utm_source=openai))
