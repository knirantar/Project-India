# Brief: US Iran War

## Bottom Line
India faces a near‑term but manageable energy and maritime shock from the 2026 U.S.–Iran war: Hormuz transits remain constrained; prices and insurance costs are elevated; yet India’s combined strategic/commercial stocks (~60 days current cover), diversified crude basket, and naval/consular posture provide buffers while New Delhi pushes for safe passage and de‑escalation. Vigilant policy coordination across MoPNG–RBI–MEA–Navy is essential until shipping normalizes. ([business-standard.com](https://www.business-standard.com/economy/news/india-has-60-days-of-fuel-stocks-govt-says-dismissing-shortage-reports-as-misinformation-126032600552_1.html?utm_source=openai))

## Key Takeaways
- Hormuz disruption is the single most important variable for India’s energy and macro stability in 2Q–3Q 2026. ([iea.org](https://www.iea.org/reports/oil-market-report-april-2026))
- India’s oil import dependence (~88% in FY25) and LNG reliance on Qatar (via Hormuz) transmit shocks to CPI/CAD; a sustained $10/bbl rise can add ~0.4% of GDP to CAD. ([indianexpress.com](https://indianexpress.com/article/business/india-oil-natural-gas-reliance-energy-demand-domestic-production-sluggish-9954570/lite/?utm_source=openai))
- War‑risk premia and convoy delays increase delivered costs and working‑capital needs for OMCs and importers; insurance remains available but expensive. ([theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/business/2026/mar/11/lloyds-of-london-insurance-shipping-strait-of-hormuz-us-israel-iran?utm_source=openai))
- Stocks and policy tools (SPR, excise/VAT levers, OMC pricing, RBI FX/liquidity backstops) can cushion a multi‑month shock; structural SPR and LNG diversification still lag exposure. ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/Pressreleaseshare.aspx?PRID=2113233&utm_source=openai))

## Strategic Context
Fresh exchanges (May 7) highlight the ceasefire’s fragility. CENTCOM’s “Project Freedom” aims to restore navigation, while IEA/EIA track a historic supply/trade shock concentrated at Hormuz. India balances Gulf ties, Chabahar interests, and exposure to sanctions/insurance while protecting diaspora and energy supply lines. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/fdc6d2ae9396377919c967746fa9996b?utm_source=openai))

## Evidence
- EIA: Hormuz 20.9 mb/d (1H 2025) and LNG transits critical to Asia. ([eia.gov](https://www.eia.gov/international/content/analysis/special_topics/World_Oil_Transit_Chokepoints/))
- IEA (Apr 2026): Record March price spike and throughput collapse; normalization hinges on Hormuz. ([iea.org](https://www.iea.org/reports/oil-market-report-april-2026))
- MEA: 11 Indian ships exited; 13 remained (May 7). ([government.economictimes.indiatimes.com](https://government.economictimes.indiatimes.com/amp/news/secure-india/11-indian-ships-exited-strait-of-hormuz-13-still-in-gulf-says-mea/130900252?utm_source=openai))
- MoPNG: ~60‑day cover and assured supplies. ([business-standard.com](https://www.business-standard.com/economy/news/india-has-60-days-of-fuel-stocks-govt-says-dismissing-shortage-reports-as-misinformation-126032600552_1.html?utm_source=openai))
- RBI/IMF: Oil pass‑through to CPI and CAD sensitivity. ([rbi.org.in](https://www.rbi.org.in/Scripts/BS_ViewBulletin.aspx?Id=23516&utm_source=openai))

## Implications
- Base case (partial reopening): Elevated but stabilizing crude; targeted domestic price smoothing; LNG tightness persists for weeks—watch fertilizer/CGD. 
- Bear case (renewed closure): Re‑acceleration to triple‑digit crude, drawdown of domestic stocks, stronger INR pressure; expanded RBI liquidity/FX operations and fiscal cushions likely.

- Quantify India’s LNG-at-risk under 30/60/90‑day Hormuz scenarios by terminal/sector; map substitution options and costs.

- Monitor convoy throughput metrics and JWC Listed Areas changes.
