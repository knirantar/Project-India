# West Bengal Elections 2026

## One-Line Summary
West Bengal voted in two phases on April 23 and 29, 2026, recording a combined turnout of 92.47%; the Election Commission of India (ECI) shows the BJP winning 207 of 293 declared seats, AITC 80, INC 2, CPI(M) 1, AISF 1, and AJUP 2, with Falta (AC-144) countermanded for a fresh poll on May 21 and result due May 24. ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2256756))

## Why This Matters
- Geopolitics: Bengal borders Bangladesh over 2,216.7 km—India’s longest international land frontier for a single state—so state leadership affects border management, migration, and trade corridors (e.g., Petrapole-Benapole). ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2158419&utm_source=openai))
- Internal growth: The state’s policy mix (large welfare schemes and MSME-heavy economy) and Centre–state dynamics shape fiscal space, investment climate, and delivery of benefits (e.g., Lakshmir Bhandar, Kanyashree, Swasthya Sathi). ([finance.wb.gov.in](https://finance.wb.gov.in/writereaddata/Budget_Speech/2024_English.pdf))
- Democratic process quality: Record participation, heavy CAPF deployment, and Supreme Court–supervised voter roll revision (SIR) make Bengal 2026 a national benchmark for election administration and rights adjudication at scale. ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2256756))

## Current State
- Schedule and turnout: ECI announced the Bengal poll schedule on March 15, 2026; Phase 1 on April 23 and Phase 2 on April 29. Phase-wise participation reached 93.19% and 91.66% respectively; combined 92.47%—a West Bengal high since Independence. Counting was set for May 4. ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2253728))
- Results status (as of May 10, 2026): BJP 207, AITC 80, INC 2, CPI(M) 1, AISF 1, AJUP 2 out of 293 declared; Falta re-poll across all 285 booths on May 21 after ECI voided the April 29 poll for “severe electoral offences.” ([results.eci.gov.in](https://results.eci.gov.in/ResultAcGenMay2026/partywiseresult-S25.htm))
- Repolls: ECI ordered repolling in 15 booths (Magrahat Paschim and Diamond Harbour) on May 2 following complaints; these do not change the overall state tally logic. ([timesofindia.indiatimes.com](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/ec-orders-repoll-in-15-booths-across-bengals-magrahat-paschim-diamond-harbour-on-may-2/articleshow/130678633.cms?utm_source=openai))
- Electors and rolls: Final roll published Feb 28, 2026 lists 6.445 crore electors (male 3.281 cr; female 3.164 cr; third gender 1,152); gender ratio improved from 956 to 964; 60.06 lakh entries remained “under adjudication” pending quasi-judicial orders for inclusion/deletion. ([wbceo.wb.gov.in](https://wbceo.wb.gov.in/Downloads/News/Final%20Press%20Note%20CEO-PN-05-2026.pdf))
- Security: The Commission and MHA staged an unprecedented security plan—initial 480 CAPF companies, later augmented; reportage indicates ~780 companies by March 31 and ~2.4 lakh personnel in total. ([indianexpress.com](https://indianexpress.com/article/cities/kolkata/bengal-2026-poll-preparations-mha-to-deploy-480-capf-companies-by-march-10-10544569/?utm_source=openai))

## Historical Context
- 2011/2016/2021: AITC ended the Left Front’s 34-year rule in 2011, retained power in 2016 and 2021 (213/294 seats in 2021). Turnout in 2021 was 82.3%. ([assembly.wb.gov.in](https://assembly.wb.gov.in/OtherPDF/LMLA.pdf))
- 2024 Lok Sabha: TMC won 29 of 42 seats in West Bengal, BJP 12—setting a competitive two-pole landscape before the 2026 Assembly contest. ([indianexpress.com](https://indianexpress.com/article/india/west-bengal-election-results-2024-live-updates-lok-sabha-bjp-tmc-congress-india-aitc-9369301/?utm_source=openai))

## Key Actors and Institutions
- Parties and leaders: AITC (Mamata Banerjee; Abhishek Banerjee), BJP (Suvendu Adhikari; state president Samik Bhattacharya since July 3, 2025), Left Front (Chair Biman Bose; CPI(M) state secretary Mohammad Salim), INC (state unit contested solo), ISF (Nawsad Siddique). ([timesofindia.indiatimes.com](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/new-west-bengal-bjp-president-samik-bhattacharya-replaces-sukanta-majumdar-unanimously-elected-state-chief/articleshow/122223958.cms?utm_source=openai))
- Election management: ECI; CEO West Bengal; Supreme Court oversight of SIR (WP(C) No. 1089/2025); extensive CAPF deployment for polls and post-poll period. ([wbceo.wb.gov.in](https://wbceo.wb.gov.in/Downloads/News/Final%20Press%20Note%20CEO-PN-05-2026.pdf))
- Border/security stakeholders: MHA; BSF South and North Bengal Frontiers along the Bangladesh border. ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2158419&utm_source=openai))

## Relevant Policies, Laws, and Programs
- Election law and conduct: RP Act, 1951, Sections 58/58A used to void polling; Model Code of Conduct; repolls ordered May 2 and Falta full re-poll on May 21. ([thestatesman.com](https://www.thestatesman.com/india/west-bengal-assembly-elections-2026-ec-orders-repolling-in-15-booths-on-may-2-1503588445.html?utm_source=openai))
- Supreme Court directions: SIR to proceed with judicial officers and revised timelines; appeals do not automatically confer voting rights if undecided by deadlines. ([api.sci.gov.in](https://api.sci.gov.in/supremecourt/2026/19822/19822_2026_1_51_69596_Order_02-Apr-2026.pdf?utm_source=openai))
- Central policy context: CAA Rules notified March 11, 2024—salient for a border state with historic migration. ([indianexpress.com](https://indianexpress.com/article/india/centre-citizenship-amendment-act-rules-notification-9208093/?utm_source=openai))
- Flagship state schemes (indicative scale FY2023–24): Lakshmir Bhandar (1.98 crore beneficiaries; Rs 10,101.87 cr spend YTD), Kanyashree (85.53 lakh cumulative beneficiaries), Rupashree (1.93 lakh in FY24), Swasthya Sathi (universal state health cover framework), Krishak Bandhu (income support, Rs 2 lakh death benefit). ([finance.wb.gov.in](https://finance.wb.gov.in/writereaddata/Budget_Speech/2024_English.pdf))

## Data and Indicators
- Electors (final roll, Feb 28, 2026): 64,445,609; Gender ratio 964; PwD electors 4,60,479; 60.06 lakh “under adjudication.” ([wbceo.wb.gov.in](https://wbceo.wb.gov.in/Downloads/News/Final%20Press%20Note%20CEO-PN-05-2026.pdf))
- Turnout: Phase 1 93.19%; Phase 2 91.66%; combined 92.47% (provisional-to-final ECI figures). ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2256756))
- Candidates: 2,926 (Phase 1: 1,478 across 152 ACs; Phase 2: 1,448 across 142 ACs). ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2251819&lang=2&reg=3))
- Polling stations: 85,377 across both phases (44,376 P1; 41,001 P2). ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2256756))
- Results (declared May 4; updated May 5): BJP 207; AITC 80; INC 2; AJUP 2; CPI(M) 1; AISF 1; Falta pending. ([results.eci.gov.in](https://results.eci.gov.in/ResultAcGenMay2026/partywiseresult-S25.htm))
- Reserved seats: SC 68; ST 16 (total ACs 294). ([assembly.wb.gov.in](https://assembly.wb.gov.in/OtherPDF/LMLA.pdf))
- Security: ~780 CAPF companies by Mar 31; ECI/MHA continued ~500 companies post-counting to deter violence. ([indianexpress.com](https://indianexpress.com/article/cities/kolkata/west-bengal-assembly-elections-2026-capf-deployment-eci-security-plan-10598859/?utm_source=openai))

## Strengths
- Extremely high and broad-based participation; gender ratio improvement in rolls; dense polling station network; 100% webcasting (per ECI) increased transparency. ([wbceo.wb.gov.in](https://wbceo.wb.gov.in/Downloads/News/Final%20Press%20Note%20CEO-PN-05-2026.pdf))
- Clear, rapidly published constituency-level results and margins on ECI portal enable granular analysis. ([results.eci.gov.in](https://results.eci.gov.in/ResultAcGenMay2026/statewiseS251.htm))

## Bottlenecks
- Voter roll SIR left 60.06 lakh entries under adjudication as of Feb 28; Supreme Court-set deadlines limited late inclusion, raising representation concerns in some areas. ([wbceo.wb.gov.in](https://wbceo.wb.gov.in/Downloads/News/Final%20Press%20Note%20CEO-PN-05-2026.pdf))
- Multiple repolls (May 2; full Falta re-poll May 21) indicate localized irregularities and administrative strain. ([timesofindia.indiatimes.com](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/ec-orders-repoll-in-15-booths-across-bengals-magrahat-paschim-diamond-harbour-on-may-2/articleshow/130678633.cms?utm_source=openai))

## Global Comparison
- Turnout rose from 84.72% (2011) and 82.3% (2016/2021 benchmark) to 92.47% in 2026—placing Bengal among the highest-turnout large-scale subnational elections globally. ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2256756))

## Opportunities
- Border governance: strengthen fencing gaps and surveillance along the 2,216.7 km WB–Bangladesh frontier; leverage Centre–state coordination to speed infrastructure and trade facilitation. ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2158419&utm_source=openai))
- Program efficiency: align high-coverage state schemes with fiscal sustainability; target leakages post-SIR with better beneficiary authentication. ([finance.wb.gov.in](https://finance.wb.gov.in/writereaddata/Budget_Speech/2024_English.pdf))

## Risks
- Post-poll law and order and partisan contestation over SIR outcomes; prolonged litigation could delay local delivery or deter investment sentiment. ([economictimes.indiatimes.com](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/elections/assembly-elections/west-bengal/ec-decides-to-keep-500-capf-companies-deployed-in-bengal-post-poll/articleshow/129998232.cms?from=mdr&utm_source=openai))
- Border vulnerabilities (riverine stretches, unfenced segments) remain security and smuggling challenges. ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2158419&utm_source=openai))

## Future Scenarios
- Consolidation: A stable BJP-led government (per ECI tallies) aligns with Centre on security, infrastructure, and CAA implementation; watch fiscal/budget reprioritization and scheme audits in FY2026–27. ([results.eci.gov.in](https://results.eci.gov.in/ResultAcGenMay2026/partywiseresult-S25.htm))
- Contention: Legal challenges and constituency-level disputes (e.g., Falta) modestly delay full assembly composition; limited policy overhang. ([m.economictimes.com](https://m.economictimes.com/news/elections/assembly-elections/west-bengal/ec-orders-fresh-polls-at-all-285-booths-in-bengals-falta-constituency/amp_articleshow/130720846.cms?utm_source=openai))
- Coexistence: High-turnout, high-security model prompts permanent upgrades to electoral operations (roll management, live webcasting, CAPF protocols) for future cycles. ([pib.gov.in](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2256756))

## What India Should Watch Next
- Falta re-poll (May 21) and result consolidation; swearing-in, portfolio allocations, and first 100-day policy signals; FY2026–27 budget priorities and any recalibration of flagship schemes. ([m.economictimes.com](https://m.economictimes.com/news/elections/assembly-elections/west-bengal/ec-orders-fresh-polls-at-all-285-booths-in-bengals-falta-constituency/amp_articleshow/130720846.cms?utm_source=openai))
